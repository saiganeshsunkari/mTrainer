Interactive testing enables you to test both the current and published versions of your app and compare their results in one screen. By default, testing runs on the current trained model only. To enable testing for a published model, you need to explicitly enable itbecause it is counted as hits and will be deducted from your key balance. If you have not published your application yet, the check box will be disabled. The test pane is on the left side of the screen and it is where you can type test utterances in the text box and press Enter to submit it to your app. To review the result of a previous utterance you typed, just click it in the test view and its result will be displayed on the right. The result view is on the right side of the screen and it is where your LUIS app returns the test result; i.e. the predicted interpretation of the utterance. In case of interactive testing on both trained and published models together, an entity may have a different prediction in each model. In the test result, this entity will be distinguished by a red underline.If you hover over the underlined entity, you can view the entity prediction in both trained and published models.
